FAQs

Frequently
Asked Questions?

1. What is DriverGuard?

DriverGuard is a software program designed to enhance driver safety. It monitors your facial expressions and eyes to detect signs of drowsiness while you're driving.

2. How does DriverGuard detect drowsiness?

DriverGuard uses a camera to capture your facial expressions. It analyzes the movement and position of your eyes. If your eyes begin to close too frequently, it sounds an alarm to alert you to stay alert.

3. What happens if DriverGuard detects drowsiness?

If DriverGuard detects that you may be getting drowsy, it triggers an alert. An alarm will sound, and you'll receive a notification.

4. How do I stop the alarm?

You can stop the alarm by pressing the 'q' key on the keyboard. This will also exit the DriverGuard program.

5. Why does DriverGuard send emails?

DriverGuard sends emails to help ensure your safety. If it detects drowsiness for an extended period or multiple times, it will send an email to a designated recipient to alert them that you may be sleepy.

6. What should I do if I receive a false alarm?

If you receive a false alarm, it's essential to stay calm and alert. False alarms can happen due to sudden movements or distractions. DriverGuard is designed to prioritize safety, so it's better to have occasional false alarms than to miss a genuine drowsiness alert.

7. Is there anything else I need to know?

It's essential to ensure that the camera is correctly positioned to capture your face. Also, make sure that the required sound files and model files are present in the specified paths. Remember, DriverGuard is designed to enhance your safety, so always pay attention to the road and prioritize safe driving practices.
